Interventions
Click on a node to intervene on it. Interventions immediately affect the target trait; they increase good traits, such as wellbeing, and decrease bad traits, such as heart disease (CHD). A key to the effects can be found in the bottom left of the screen.

Goals
Use the reset button in the top left to reload the visualisation and see the effects of different interventions. You are free to use this visualisation however you like, but you might want to try:
  • Hover over a trait to see its name and prevalence
  • Intervene on education and see that it has far-reaching effects
  • Intervene on BMI and seeing that when it is reduced heart disease (CHD) also reduces
  • Make multiple interventions at the same time and see their interacting effects
Help
Troubleshooting
Some traits may be unfamiliar to you, you aren't expected to know about every one but here are some definitions:
  • Eveningness: Whether you are a 'morning' or 'evening' person
  • CHD: Short for Chronic Heart Disease
  • Neuroticism: Personality trait associated with seeing the world as distressing and more often feeling strong emotions such as worry and anger
  • BMI: Your weight (relative to your height)
  • Wellbeing: How happy you report yourself as feeling
  • Depression: Mental health disorder associated with unhappiness, loss of motivation, and negative cognitive biases
Some effects are really tiny, so we represent this accurately (if they're tiny then they won't matter much)! You can try increasing the zoom on your browser and refreshing the page, or dragging the traits to reposition them on the page
